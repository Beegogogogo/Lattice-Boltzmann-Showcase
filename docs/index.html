Lattice Boltzmann Solver

From LBM solver validation to application-focused flow showcases

This site presents a lattice Boltzmann solver through a connected progression: first the canonical benchmark cases that verify core numerical behavior in 2D and 3D, then application-facing showcases that extend the same solver toward medical flow, reactive transport, crystallization, and particle-laden dynamics.

Application Directions

Four research-facing flow domains

Medical flow

Hemodynamic simulations in patient-specific or idealized vascular geometries, including aneurysm and artery-scale flow structures.

Chemical reaction

Reactive transport and coupled scalar-evolution cases for showing how the solver extends beyond pure hydrodynamics.

Crystallization

Phase-change and crystal-growth simulations for presenting interfacial evolution, transport coupling, and pattern formation.

Particle flows

Particle-laden and collision-driven flow problems that highlight multiphase motion, interaction dynamics, and suspended-body transport.

Application Showcase

Four application directions for results presentation

Beyond the canonical benchmarks, the solver is positioned to support application-facing demonstrations in medicine, reactive transport, crystallization, and particle-laden flows. This section frames how those results can be presented.

Medical flow

Show hemodynamic simulations with velocity fields, pressure distributions, and near-wall transport patterns in vascular geometries. The next result block is organized into seven concrete showcase directions.

Showcase 01

Idealized aneurysm

Present vortex development, impingement zones, and wall-shear patterns in simplified aneurysm geometries.

Showcase 02

Patient specific aneurysm

Present flow structures, impingement regions, and wall-shear distributions in reconstructed patient-specific aneurysm geometries.

Showcase 03

Blood non-Newtonian behavior

Compare shear-thinning rheology against Newtonian flow to show viscosity-dependent changes in recirculation and near-wall transport.

Showcase 04

Stent

Highlight how implanted struts alter local flow organization, streamline redistribution, and hemodynamic exposure near the vessel wall.

Showcase 05

Hemodynamic indicators

Summarize derived quantities such as wall shear stress, oscillatory shear behavior, residence-time proxies, and other flow-informed biomarkers.

Showcase 06

Larynx simulation

Present airway and laryngeal flow structures with coupled jet formation, recirculation regions, and transport features relevant to vocal-tract dynamics.

Showcase 07

Thrombosis simulation

Show thrombus-growth or clot-formation scenarios by coupling flow evolution with platelet transport, activation, and accumulation patterns.

Chemical reaction

Present coupled flow and reaction cases through concentration contours, mixing fronts, and time-resolved evolution of reactive species.

Crystallization

Highlight nucleation and crystal-growth behavior with evolving interfaces, morphology changes, and transport-limited pattern formation.

Particle flows

Display suspended-particle transport, collision events, and multiphase trajectories to illustrate interaction-dominated flow dynamics.
